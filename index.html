School of Excellence and Green Ribbon School.
Why is 2 Rivers Great?

Two Rivers has been a STEM school since its inception in 2002, recognized as a School of Excellence by Magnet Schools of America, as well as being named a 2016 Green Ribbon School by the U.S. Department of Education for our environmental programs and practices. However, our strengths extend far beyond the areas of science, technology, engineering and math. Below are highlights of the many interests and talents of our student body and staff.



A School that Helps the Environment.
How does Two Rivers Help the Environment?

An environmentally themed STEM school, TRMS focuses on environmental awareness through courses like sustainability, environmental ethics, and a field science class. Our focus on the environment extends beyond the classroom as well. Our recycling program is run through our students, who collect recyclables from classrooms and collect data on how classes are doing in terms of accuracy. Our school widely supports recycling; we fill 15-20 ninety gallon barrels of single stream recycling per week. After a small scale compost operation last year, we have introduced a schoolwide composting program to Two Rivers. Our kitchen staff collects any waste produced in the kitchen, and our sixth graders are piloting a student compost program, in which students collect food scraps during lunch for compost. On average, we collect three 90-gallon barrels of food scraps weekly.

what is new?

  • Aquaculture has come back.

    Students will learn “by doing” in a our very popular Dynamic Careers in Aquaculture Interdistrict Grant (DCA). This program enrolls students from several neighboring school districts and focuses on the scientific, ecological, economic, and career aspects of the field of aquaculture. Its hands-on activities will provide exciting opportunities for students to acquire practical knowledge, “fun” relaxed atmosphere and learn about aquaculture in Connecticut by visiting local businesses raising shellfish and produce.

  • Students can now participate is the "Math Video Challenge".

    Mrs. Vega's math students took part in a fun national challenge put forth by MathCounts. The Math Video Challenge is designed for groups of four and "inspires creativity in students through project-based learning..."
    Through a math video, students are encouraged to make connections to their own lives and teach others a math concept in the process.
